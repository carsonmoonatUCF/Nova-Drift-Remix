In Binary Break In you take control of a computer virus attacking a computer 
server.

Basic Controls: 
Movement: Press A or Right Mouse Click to move the player forward towards the mouse cursor
Attack: Hold S or Left Mouse Click to fire projectiles in front of the player.

Health and Shield:
The shield bar is depleted when hit or collided into. After a few seconds the shield will regenerate.
After the shield is depleted damage goes towards Health. Health cannot be regenerated. Losing all health
results in a game over.

UPGRADES
Turret: Press Z to place a turret
Halo: Passive ability that protects player
Blink: Press x to teleport a short distance in the direction the player is facing

Basic Gameplay Requirements:

Binary Break In meets all the requirements for basic gameplay
 -2 Levels are adapted from the game. Since the original game is wave based, we
  took an early wave and a boss fight
 -Music plays on all stages of the game including main menus, and levels
 -Sound Effects are used in the player projectile, and enemy damage, and player damage
 -Gameplay meets the 60-90 second target

Art Requirements:

 -All art was made by the team
 -Sprites are used for background, player, enemy, projectile, and boss
 -Animation is used for the background of level 2
 -Particle effects are used for the cookie enemy death and player trail
 -Menus contain logos and general aesthetics that tie in with the rest of the game

Code Requirements:

 -All code was made by team members
 -The game has not been found to crash
 -The game supports keyboard and mouse
 -The significant mechanics of the game are shooting, upgrades, screen wrapping
  enemy wave spawn, and boss battle

Tech Requirements:
 -All levels were made by team members
 -Two levels resemble the original game
 -The game contains a main menu, about menu, loss and win menus
 -There are no dead ends in UI or in levels

Other Elements Requirements:
 -The world construction inhabits a digital landscape that exists inside a computer
 -Progression is made through defeating enemies and selecting upgrades
 -There are two main enemies, one that follows the player and one that floats in one direction
 -Sound effects and background music work and match the general aesthetic
